# Compass code of conduct

Compass aims to maintain a respectful, constructive community where people can use, discuss, and improve the project without harassment or intimidation.

## Expected behavior

Contributors and maintainers must:

- Discuss ideas and technical disagreements with patience and respect
- Critique work and decisions without attacking people
- Welcome questions from people with different backgrounds and experience levels
- Give credit, respect privacy, and correct mistakes openly
- Stop disruptive behavior when a maintainer asks

## Unacceptable behavior

The project doesn't accept:

- Harassment, threats, stalking, intimidation, or deliberate disruption
- Discrimination or derogatory comments based on identity or personal characteristics
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome attention
- Publication of private information without permission
- Repeated personal attacks, trolling, or bad-faith participation
- Retaliation against someone who reports a concern

## Scope

This policy applies to issues, pull requests, Discussions, reviews, project events, and other spaces where someone represents Compass. It also applies to private communication about project work.

## Report conduct concerns

Use GitHub's **Report content** control on the relevant issue, pull request, comment, or discussion. Select **Report to repository admins** when GitHub offers that option. GitHub documents the available paths in [Reporting abuse or spam](https://docs.github.com/en/communities/maintaining-your-safety-on-github/reporting-abuse-or-spam).

Don't use a public issue to disclose private details about a conduct incident. Security advisories are reserved for product vulnerabilities.

## Enforcement

Maintainers will consider the context, impact, and pattern of reported behavior. They may edit or remove content, issue a warning, close an interaction, restrict participation, or escalate a report to GitHub.

Maintainers must apply this policy in good faith and avoid conflicts of interest. A maintainer involved in an incident should not make the final enforcement decision when another maintainer can review it.
